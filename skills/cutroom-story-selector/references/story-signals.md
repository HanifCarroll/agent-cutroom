# Story Signals

Use these signals to find short-video candidates inside transcripts, walk notes, or review windows.

## Strong Signals

- `contrast`: before/after, expected/actual, common belief/real problem.
- `lesson`: a specific mistake, decision, or rule the viewer can reuse.
- `proof`: concrete result, artifact, number, test, or observed behavior.
- `stakes`: why the issue matters now, especially time, money, quality, or credibility.
- `turn`: the speaker changes direction, reframes the problem, or lands the real point.
- `specific example`: named workflow, tool, customer, repo, clip, or failure.
- `clean phrase`: a spoken sentence that can act as the clip's opening line without rewriting.

## Weak Signals

- Abstract summary with no example.
- Setup that never reaches a point.
- Interesting aside that depends on missing context.
- Generic advice with no speaker-owned proof.
- Good transcript text attached to unusable visual or audio quality.

## Candidate Shape

A selected story should usually fit this shape:

1. Hook: the first line creates a reason to keep watching.
2. Claim: the speaker states the useful point.
3. Turn: the point becomes sharper or more surprising.
4. Proof: an example, artifact, result, or concrete workflow.
5. Payoff: the viewer understands the lesson or next action.
